Interactive Brokers

Interactive Brokers is a major global online brokerage firm primarily known for its trading platform, tools, and services. The firm is based in Greenwich, Connecticut, and operates the largest electronic trading platform in the U.S. by number of daily average revenue trades.

The firm offers a broad range of services, including trading in stocks, options, futures, forex, bonds, and funds. Interactive Brokers caters to several types of users: individual investors, institutional investors, financial advisors, and broker-dealers.

The company has been noted for its robust trading platform called Trader Workstation (TWS). TWS is designed for active traders and institutions and provides a wealth of features, including advanced order types, algorithmic trading, risk management tools, and a comprehensive charting package. There's also a Web-based version and a mobile app, providing flexibility for users with different needs or preferences.

Moreover, Interactive Brokers is also known for its competitive fees and commission structures, which are particularly attractive for high-volume traders. The firm often has low margin rates as well.

One of the distinctive features of Interactive Brokers is its global reach. It provides direct market access to stocks, options, futures, forex, bonds, and funds in over 135 markets in numerous countries around the world.

The company's extensive offerings and complex platform make it a go-to for serious traders and investors, although it may be a bit overwhelming for beginners who are new to investing.

Tradestation

TradeStation is a leading online brokerage firm known for its analysis software, electronic trading platform, and trading technology. As of my last knowledge update in September 2021, it's based in Plantation, Florida.

TTradeStation offers various services such as trading in stocks, options, futures, and cryptocurrencies. It caters to different types of traders ranging from individual active traders to institutional traders.

The key feature of TradeStation is its advanced and robust trading platform that provides high-speed order execution, real-time market data, and fully customizable charts and analysis tools. It offers three different trading platforms: TradeStation 10 (desktop-based), Web Trading (web-based), and TS GO (mobile-based). These platforms offer users a variety of trading tools, including radar screens for market monitoring, advanced charting and back-testing capabilities, and algorithmic trading strategies.

The company is known for its competitive pricing structure. TradeStation has made significant strides to make its pricing more accessible, including offering commission-free trades on stocks and ETFs.

TradeStation's EasyLanguage programming language is a distinguishing feature. It allows traders to create their custom trading strategies, indicators, and order types. It's less complex than many other programming languages, opening up strategy development to a wider array of traders.

TradeStation also provides educational resources for traders, including webinars, video tutorials, and articles to help users understand the platform and enhance their trading knowledge.

As with any brokerage, the specifics of TradeStation's offerings, pricing, and features may have changed post-September 2021, and it would be wise to visit TradeStation's website or contact their customer service for the most recent updates.
